## Critical Findings

### PESSOAL: Monolithic Principle Violated ❌

**Problem:** Two disconnected ovals instead of one unified leaf shape.

**Impact:** 
- Reads as "8" or abstract symbol at 28px (NOT recognizable as leaf)
- Fails monolithic form requirement (violates core visual DNA)
- Too geometric, not organic
- Does NOT pass visual gravity test (would NOT appear on protest banner)

**Recommendation:** Complete redesign needed.

### WORK: Production Ready ✅

**Status:** APPROVED  
**Strengths:**
- ✅ Instantly readable at 28px
- ✅ Monolithic form (head + handle unified)
- ✅ Strong visual presence (protest-banner worthy)
- ✅ Organic hand-drawn aesthetic
- ✅ Perfect political movement aesthetic

**Action:** NO CHANGES NEEDED

### PROJECTS: Needs Organic Refinement ⚠️

**Problem:** Too geometric, lacks hand-drawn feel.

**Impact:**
- Readable at 28px (good)
- Monolithic form (good)
- Visual boldness (good)
- BUT: Feels digitally-generated (perfectly symmetric sharp angles)

**Recommendation:** Soften angles with micro-curves, add asymmetry to suggest organic hand-drawing (30-minute refinement).

## Key Lessons Learned

1. **Monolithic form is non-negotiable** — Two separate shapes break the political movement aesthetic
2. **Organic feel matters** — Perfect geometry = feels corporate, not revolutionary
3. **28px readability is critical** — If it doesn't read at small size, it fails
4. **Visual gravity test works** — "Would this be on a protest banner?" is the perfect question
